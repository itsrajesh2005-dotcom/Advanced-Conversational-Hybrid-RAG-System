# 1. Introduction to Artificial Intelligence

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is a branch of computer science that focuses on creating systems capable of performing tasks that normally require human intelligence.

These tasks include:

- Learning
- Problem-solving
- Decision-making
- Language understanding
- Image recognition
- Speech recognition
- Reasoning

AI systems analyze data, identify patterns, and make predictions or decisions.

## Goals of AI

- Automate repetitive tasks
- Improve efficiency
- Reduce manual work
- Enhance decision-making
- Solve complex problems faster

## Characteristics of AI

- Intelligence
- Learning capability
- Adaptability
- Automation
- Data-driven decisions

# 2. History of AI

## Early Stage

- 1950: Alan Turing introduced the Turing Test.
- 1956: The term Artificial Intelligence was introduced at Dartmouth Conference.

## AI Winters

AI development slowed due to:

- Lack of computing power
- Limited datasets
- High costs

## Modern AI Boom

AI grew rapidly because of:

- Big data
- Powerful GPUs
- Cloud computing
- Deep learning

# 3. Types of AI

## Based on Capability

### 1. Narrow AI

AI designed for specific tasks.

Examples:
- Siri
- Alexa
- Chatbots
- Recommendation systems

### 2. General AI

AI capable of performing any intellectual task like humans.

Currently theoretical.

### 3. Super AI

AI more intelligent than humans.

Still hypothetical.

## Based on Functionality

### Reactive Machines

- No memory
- Respond only to current input

### Limited Memory

- Uses past data temporarily

### Theory of Mind

- Understands emotions and beliefs

### Self-Aware AI

- AI with consciousness

# 26. Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG)

RAG combines retrieval systems with LLMs.

## Workflow

1. User asks question
2. Retriever fetches relevant chunks
3. LLM generates answer

## Advantages

- Reduces hallucination
- Uses custom documents
